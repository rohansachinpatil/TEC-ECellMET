About the Competition

The Technical Excellence Challenge (TEC) is a flagship entrepreneurship competition organized by the Entrepreneurship Cell (E-Cell) designed to identify, nurture, and promote innovative ideas and entrepreneurial talent among college students.

TEC provides a comprehensive platform where aspiring entrepreneurs can transform their ideas into viable business solutions through a structured 3-month program. The competition brings together students from multiple colleges, creating a vibrant ecosystem of innovation, collaboration, and learning.

Through multiple rounds of evaluation, mentorship sessions, and real-world challenges, participants gain invaluable experience in ideation, market research, product development, business planning, and pitching to investors.

Our Objectives

Foster Innovation

Encourage students to think creatively and develop innovative solutions to real-world problems.

Build Entrepreneurial Skills

Provide hands-on experience in business development, market analysis, and startup methodology.

Create Networking Opportunities

Connect students with mentors, industry professionals, and fellow entrepreneurs.

Promote Startup Culture

Cultivate an entrepreneurial mindset and startup culture in academic institutions.

Rules & Guidelines

  1. Team Composition: Each team must have a minimum of 2 members and a maximum of 5 members.
  2. Registration: Only one team leader should register the team. All member details must be provided during registration.
  3. Submissions: All submissions must be made before the specified deadlines. Late submissions will not be accepted.
  4. Originality: All ideas and work submitted must be original. Plagiarism will lead to immediate disqualification.
  5. Team Changes: Team member changes are allowed only during the registration phase. No changes permitted after Round 1 begins.
  6. Communication: All official communication will be done through the registered email address. Check your email regularly.
  7. Evaluation: Decisions made by the evaluation committee will be final and binding.
  8. Code of Conduct: All participants must maintain professional behavior and respect towards organizers, mentors, and fellow participants.
